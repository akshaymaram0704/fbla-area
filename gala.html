Important Details

Welcome to our Nature-Themed Harvest Gala. Join us at Hyatt Regency Chicago(151 E Wacker Dr, Chicago, IL 60601) February 16th at 4:00pm - 10:00pm, looking forward to seeing you all there!

Opening Ceremony & Nature Welcome

Get Excited for the grand opening ceremony, featuring a warm welcome to nature. Enjoy a refreshing nature-inspired drink and hors d'oeuvres as you explore the venue.

Art Gallery Showcase & Food Tasting

Explore the breathtaking art gallery featuring nature-inspired creations from talented artists. Indulge in a delightful food tasting experience with a menu inspired by the harvest season. Dance to live music and celebrate creativity and culinary delights.

Guest Speakers & Interactive Workshops

Indulge in the creativity of our featured artists and designers. Join food insecurity talks led by renowned guest speakers, craft nature-inspired creations in workshops. Enjoy a buffet dinner with a variety of dishes incorporating locally sourced, seasonal ingredients.

Testimonials, Recognition & Live Auction

Listen to heartwarming testimonials from our supporters and contributors. Recognize the efforts of individuals who have made significant contributions to the success of our gala. Get ready for an exciting live and silent auction sponsored by our partners with a variety of items and experiences.

Location

Welcome to our spectacular venue, the hub of excitement for our upcoming gala event! Nestled at 151 E Wacker Dr, Chicago, IL 60601, our location is not just an address; it's an experience waiting to unfold.

Guest Speakers

Special feautures in our program

Raj Patel

Birtish activist, journalist

Patel has given talks addressing the systemic issues contributing to food insecurity. He emphasizes the global economic and political factors that lead to unequal access to food, discussing issues such as land distribution, corporate control over agriculture, and the impact of globalization on food systems.

Ellen Gustafson

American businessperson, social entrepreneur

Gustafson's talks often focus on the interconnectedness of global food challenges and the need for a more sustainable and equitable food system. She explores the role of agriculture, trade, and policy in addressing hunger and advocates for a holistic approach to food security.

Danielle Nierenberg

President and Co-Founder of Food Tank

Nierenberg's talks frequently center on innovative solutions to food insecurity and the importance of sustainable agriculture. She highlights successful projects and initiatives around the world that address hunger, promote food justice, and encourage environmentally friendly farming practices.
